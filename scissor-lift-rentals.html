Where scissor lifts are used

Typical work that suits scissor lifts best

Scissor lifts are chosen when you need straight-up access, a predictable deck and space for both people and materials.

Interior fit-outs and finishing

Use scissor lifts for services and finishes at ceiling level in offices, malls and commercial buildings. The deck serves as a moving platform for repeated work in each bay.

  • Ceiling grids, ducting and sprinklers
  • Lighting, speakers and displays
  • Signage and decorative elements

Warehouses and logistics hubs

In storage and distribution facilities, scissor lifts help with racking, high-bay equipment and safety systems without disturbing floor operations more than necessary.

  • Racking and guardrail installation
  • Fire detection and cabling above aisles
  • Planned inspections in high-bay zones

Industrial and plant maintenance

Plants and workshops use scissor lifts around production lines, platforms and utility corridors where the primary need is safe vertical access, not horizontal outreach.

A stable work area that repeats every cycle.

Once in position, the scissor lift cycles up and down to the same working level, allowing teams to repeat tasks with minimal setup each time.

Platform styles and environments

Match scissor lifts to your floor conditions

Scissor lifts are selected based on where they will travel and work. The same project might use compact slab units inside and larger units on external areas.

Slab scissor lifts

  • Designed for smooth, level floors and well-defined travel paths.
  • Compact dimensions for movement through doors and between aisles.
  • Non-marking tyres to protect finished and polished surfaces.

Rough-terrain scissor lifts

  • Taller units with higher ground clearance for outdoor work zones.
  • Suited for construction yards, open slabs and plant exteriors.
  • Wide decks with space for tools and two or more technicians.

Confirm that the route from unloading point to work area is free of low beams and tight corners. Check that floors are firm and level within the limits recommended for the planned scissor lift. Plan a clear zone around the platform at height so tools and material can be handled safely. Brief operators and ground staff on how movements and positioning will be communicated.
